Academic Qualifications

Leaving Certificate


The Leaving Certificate examination is the Irish Equivalent of the A-Level. It is a two-year course that is taken by all students in Ireland. The Leaving Certificate is a requirement for entry to third-level education in Ireland.

In the LC, I studied the three core subjects of English, Irish and Maths, as well as German, Music, Physics and Geography.

Computer Science (International)

Computer Science (International) is a four-year course in TU Dublin's modern Grangegorman Campus.

The course gives students a fundamental understanding of the principles of computer science, as well as the ability to apply these principles to solve real-world problems. Unlike other courses, this course adds a core module on a language (German, Chinese, or Korean) as well as modules on globalism and software in the context of international inclusion.

In your third year, you can choose to study or work abroad for a semester. Students of Computer Science (International) will graduate with a BSc in Computer Science and a BA in their language.

Certifications and Awards

IRISSCON CTF

In early November of 2022, I participated in the IRISSCON CTF, a Capture the Flag competition hosted by IRISSCERT and Honeynet, this was my second CTF.

Throughout the day, my team and I solved a variety of challenges, ranging from simple web challenges to more complex reverse engineering challenges, infiltration and general knowledge, we competed against a variety of other teams, ranging from fellow college students studying Cybersecurity to professionals in the field. Despite being first-year students, we competed well and came second overall.

Zerodays CTF


Zerodays is a Capture the Flag competition that gameifies cybersecurity. I attended their TU Dublin event hosted by the Ethical Hackers Society, this was my first ever CTF, and I had a great time.

My team and I competed against teams of other students from TU Dublin. Tasks involved reverse-engineering, web exploits, cryptography, lock picking and other fun tasks. Over the course of a few hours we solved many challenges and came first by a large margin.

Because of this, we won tickets to IRISSCERT which allowed us to compete in the IRISSCON CTF.

TU Dublin Hackathon


The TU Dublin President's Sustainability Challenge was a hackathon hosted by TU Dublin's Growthhub and sponsored by Workday, the hackathon centered around the theme of minimising food waste in Ireland.

The competition was open to all students of TU Dublin and was a great opportunity to work with other students on a project that could have a real impact on the world.

My team, consisting of myself and three other students created a solution which came fourth overall in the competition.

AIST Stagepass


The Association of Irish Stage Technicians is an organisation which aims to improve safety and profeciency in the stage and event industry. I am a member of AIST and have completed their Stagepass course.

A Stagepass card-holder is a person who knows how to safely and efficiently work in technical theatre environments. Similar to the 'Safepass', the Stagepass curriculum focuses on electrical, manual-handling, and operational safety.

I successfully completed the AIST Stagepass course in November 2021.

Clarinet Grade 4


I have been playing clarinet since 2016, having started in my school's wind ensemble, over the years I have studied for and passed the ABRSM exams, to Grade 4, which I completed in 2019.

ABRSM is the Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music, it is a UK-based music examination board that offers internationally recognised exams and grades in a variety of instruments and genres.

Currently I am studying for my Grade 6 exam, which I hope to complete in 2023.

Poetry Aloud


Poetry Aloud is a competition ran by the National Library of Ireland and Poetry Ireland, it is a competition for secondary school students to recite poetry.

Students are marked on oration, and memorisation of two poems, one prescribed and the other chosen by the competitor.

I competed from 2016 - 2019 and was the only Templeogue College student to progress to the semi-finals, which I did in 2018, and 2019.
